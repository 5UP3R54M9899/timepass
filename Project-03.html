The Hero Association




The Hero Association (ヒーロー協会, Hīrō Kyōkai) is an organization that manages all of the cities' heroes.
It operates independently of the government.
It works hand-in-hand with the National Hero Registry (ヒーロー名簿, Hīrō meibo), the database that embraces every recognized hero.
The Hero Association uses a hierarchical class system to organize its heroes, with four classes and individually numbered ranks within respective classes (classes C, B, A, and S).




Hero Entrance Exam


To become an official hero of the Hero Association, a candidate must pass an entrance exam.

The entrance exam consists of two parts:

Fitness Test - The fitness portion of the exam tests the physical capabilities of the candidate, such as agility, reflexes, and physical strength.
The components of this exam include side-to-side jumps (for 30 seconds), a 1500 meter run, weight-lifting, squat jumps, shot put, whack-a-mole, and punching machine components.

Written Test - The written test is designed to assess the candidate's "sense of justice". It also has an essay section.

Passing the Entrance Exam has the following actions:
  1. A hero is officially recognized by the Hero Association.
  2. A hero is entitled to monetary compensation in proportion to the amount of hero work he or she does.
  3. An "ability" rank will be assigned based on the results of the exam (see "Classes" section below).
  4. A hero will be eligible to be voted upon by the public in the hero popularity ranking.

Upon reaching the A-Class:

Every A-Class hero needs to conduct a teacher seminar after new heroes are registered.

Classes



Based on various criteria, heroes are categorized into four different classes.
The C-Class is associated as composing of the weakest heroes, while the S-Class is recognized as composing of the strongest heroes:

S-Class, with a total of 17 heroes
A-Class, with a total of 38 heroes
B-Class, with a total of 101 heroes
C-Class, with a total of 390 heroes
